<p><p>The nasal spray flu vaccine market comprises two main types: trivalent and quadrivalent flu vaccines. Trivalent vaccines protect against three influenza virus strains: two A strains and one B strain. In contrast, quadrivalent vaccines offer broader protection against four strains, adding a second B strain to the formulation. The choice between these vaccines often depends on regional guidelines, patient age, and health status, with quadrivalent vaccines generally preferred for their enhanced coverage against circulating flu strains.</p></p>
