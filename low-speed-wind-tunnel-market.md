<p><p>The low-speed wind tunnel market consists of two primary types: vertical and horizontal wind tunnels. Vertical wind tunnels are designed for testing in an upward airflow orientation, providing advantages for certain aerodynamic studies and accessible pilot simulators. In contrast, horizontal wind tunnels focus on airflow along a flat plane, commonly used for vehicle and aircraft testing. Each type serves distinct applications in research and development, ensuring accurate aerodynamic assessments in various engineering fields.</p></p>
