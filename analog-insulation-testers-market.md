<p><p>Analog insulation testers measure the resistance of insulation in electrical systems using either AC or DC voltage. AC voltage testers apply alternating current, helping to identify insulation breakdown under operational conditions, while DC voltage testers utilize direct current, ideal for detecting faults in low-capacitance systems. Both types serve critical roles in ensuring electrical safety and integrity. The choice between AC and DC depends on the specific application, ensuring comprehensive testing across various environments and insulation types.</p></p>
